About

Welcome to the project

  • 2009-2011

    Our Humble Beginnings

    It is difficult for homeless people to search for a job, given that it takes an enormous amount of time to get enough resources just to survive for a day. For example, homeless people spend a lot of time standing in lines, waiting for food at the food bank several times a day, waiting for a bed at the shelter, for other public services, or standing on street corners asking for alms to buy food and necessities. Eliminating waiting time at public services would help them to have more time to look for a job and get off the streets faster. This keeps homeless people out of our crowded prisons, producing useful goods and services for our economy, instead of being a burden to taxpayers and charities. We propose an improved queuing system to be used by social services and their customers to allow customers to spend a smaller amount of time in lines.

  • March 2011

    An Agency is Born

    We plan to create a software database system which gathers RFID tag information from customers to provide a time to return and obtain services, provide denial of services when capacity has been reached, a warning of how much capacity is left, and save customers’ time for other activities. We will construct a prototype system using an RFID tag reader and tags, and a display to show information pertaining to customers. We will survey users for the usefulness of this system, conduct field analysis of alternative systems currently in use.

  • December 2012

    Transition to Full Service

    As a result of the project, engineers will have a model for designing systems in the field for managing queues. They could use this technology for other uses. For instance, a similar system could be used for managing queues in amusement parks, launch events for new products, public transportation, movie night launches, etc.
